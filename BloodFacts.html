    • What is Blood?

    Blood is the lifeline of the human body—it’s a fluid connective tissue that
    delivers oxygen, nutrients, hormones, and essential substances to cells
    while removing waste products like carbon dioxide.
    It plays a crucial role in immune defense, healing, and temperature regulation.


    Components of Blood:

    Blood is made up of four main components:

    Red Blood Cells (RBCs) – Carry oxygen to tissues and remove carbon dioxide

    White Blood Cells (WBCs) – Defend the body against infections

    Platelets – Help in blood clotting and wound healing

    Plasma – The liquid part that transports nutrients, hormones, and waste

    Blood Types

    Blood types are classified based on the ABO system and the Rh factor. These determine compatibility for transfusions and organ donations.

    ABO Blood Group System

    This system categorizes blood into four main types based on the presence of specific antigens on red blood cells and antibodies in the plasma.

    Type A

    • Antigens on red blood cells: A antigen
    • Antibodies in plasma: Anti-B

    Type B

    • Antigens on red blood cells: B antigen
    • Antibodies in plasma: Anti-A

    Type AB

    • Antigens on red blood cells: A and B antigens
    • Antibodies in plasma: None

    Type O

    • Antigens on red blood cells: None
    • Antibodies in plasma: Anti-A and Anti-B

    Rh Factor (+ or -)

    In addition to ABO classification, blood types are further categorized based on the Rh factor,
    which refers to the Rh antigen (D antigen).


  • Rh-positive (Rh+):Has the Rh antigen.
  • Rh-negative (Rh-):Lacks the Rh antigen.

    • Blood type compatibility depends on both the ABO and Rh factor:

  • Rh-negative individuals can only receive Rh-negative blood.
  • Rh-positive individuals can receive both Rh-positive and Rh-negative blood.
